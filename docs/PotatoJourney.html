Nah pls just take me to the recipe


The prompt from Emily:

Please create a slide sharing

1) a recipe of a potato product you have never eaten before,

2) your version after making it (including award-winning pictures),

3) your extensive review after trying it

What immediately came to mind at first consideration of the potatompt (potato + prompt = potatompt) was the difficulty of the first condition. “A recipe of a potato product you have never eaten before” As a fellow potato enjoyer, I didn’t think there were a lot of potato dishes I haven’t tried yet. I did a search online for ideas and came across List of Potato Dishes Wikipedia Page  and started scrolling down the list.

‼️There were in fact, some potato dishes I had not eaten yet. The most interesting one from this list is Okroshka , a Russian cold soup of raw vegetables, boiled potato, and cooked beef. What’s interesting is that the example vegetables listed (cucumber, radish, spring onions) were all veggies I would think of as more fresh/juicy spring veggies with a crunch. But as a cold soup?? I personally am not a fan of cold soups so I think this dish although I have never tried, I don’t really want to make for this potatompt. The other dishes that drew my attention were similar though. All european dishes that blended vegetables, potato, and meat together. There is Rumbledethumps , a Scottish dish of potato, cabbage and onion. Colcannon , an Irish dish of potatoes mashed with butter and milk, with chopped up cabbage and pot herbs. Trinxat , a Catalan good of potato, cabbage, and pork meat mashed and sliced together. There’s a theme here. I haven’t had these kinds of potato dish before. There’s definitely things I have had that are similar like a beef sheppards pie and generally potato croquettes… wait… I also wanna learn how to fry croquettes… so I am gonna kinda invent my own dish based on these three european dishes I mentioned before but then turn it into a croquette because I wanna learn to deep fry in a pan. If it’s my own dish, then it should qualify as a “potato product I haven’t eaten before”. I haven't had a croquette that has cabbage blended into it, that could be fun. So the result would be like these dishes but maybe American-fied with the frying. But if I had to describe it, I think it’s probably the most like a Rumbledethump. So this dish is a fusion of Rumbledethump + croquette. I used this recipe for croquettes  and remixed it.

Did you know different cultures tend to make different shapes for croquettes? Wikipedia has a cool list of the differences!

The possibilities are endless though, I could put anything into it as long as the base contained potato and cabbage!

After a lengthy 2.72 minutes of deliberation, I decided the ingredients would be:

Potato, cabbage, green onion, leek, and a salmon core.

I do not have flour though, and I didn’t want to buy a whole bag just for this. So I pulled the classic “Can I borrow a cup of flour?” move from my neighbor. Thank you Neil lol. Extremely non sus bag of flour pictured. Banana for scale.

As I started making this dish, I started making some modifications. (as cooking goes)

Using a nutribullet for blending the potatoes and veggies made it turn kinda goopy. I am unsure why but possibly I needed to let the potatoes dry HAHA. I didn’t really wait. Taste test was really yummy though. So to make it less sus looking I had a few leftover cooked veggies that I finely chopped into the mix after blending.

           

When I went to go buy potatoes, I forgot to pick up breadcrumbs. Although QFC is literally 1 block away, I thought to myself, nah, I can probably use something I have to do the same thing. I thought to make it more interesting, why don’t I use crushed potato chips as the breadcrumb instead? I bought this the other day. I thought it would add a fun taste since the chips are a bit sweet!

Now it was ball time.

Ok so the goopy-ness def came back to bite me. From the pictures, you might see how my croquettes were succumbing to gravity and to non-cohesive-ness. I have a new hypothesis now. I think in addition to drying out the potatoes, the blended leeks made the potato mash more goopy than it should have been. 🤔Since leeks do have a kinda slimy texture when cooked, it's possible that due to some food chemistry the potatoes also became a bit slimy too. But that’s just a theory… a game theory

Frying did not help. I didn’t want to buy more oil so I didn’t quite have enough oil to do a deep fry and so things kinda became panfried potato as the croquettes became flat. You can see the exposed salmon cores lol.

The Actual Recipe

Jayleen’s Rumbledethump Croquettes

?? servings

Ingredients:

  • ~1.75 pounds Russet potato (3 potatoes)
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 3 stalks green onion
  • A handful of green cabbage (avoid stalky parts)
  • 6 chives stalks
  • Smoked salmon
  • Salt to taste (or garlic salt)
  • Egg (for breading)
  • Breadcrumbs or crushed potato chips in this case
  • Flour (for breading)
  • Oil (for frying)

  1. Chop leek, green onion, cabbage into smaller pieces. Doesn’t need to be too small since I'm gonna use a nutribullet later. If you don’t have a nutribullet, you’re gonna have to fine chop those bad bois.
  2. Cube potato and boil salted water for 15 minutes until fork tender, drain and let them steam off dry out
  3. Put 3 tablespoon butter and cook the veggies until tender (add a bit of salt too). The ratio of veggies is up to you. I just used the instincts of my ancestors.
  4. Mash potato, butter, salt, green onion, cabbage, leek with a blender or by hand.
  1. I left some unblended veggies and chopped it to add to mash since nutribullet made it look like goop lol
  1. Place mash into fridge, leave overnight or until cooled down
  2. (After cooled down) Cut smoked salmon into cubes for the cores for the croquettes.
  3. Beat 1 egg (this will be used for breading)
  4. Whatever size your heart desires but I think half an inch cubes are a good size.
  5. Create croquette balls or whatever shape of your liking around the salmon cores.
  6. Roll in flour, then egg, then in breadcrumbs(potato chips)
  7. Fill your choice of cookware with oil for frying.
  8. Fry those baddies
  9. Place on a towel or paper towel to let cool/absorb oil
  10. CONSUME


Review

The Rumbledethump Croquettes tasted pretty good but were overly oily. Cooking is always a learning process indeed. The cause for the oiliness was I used chips for the breading instead of actual breadcrumbs, so I probably should have used less oil or even no oil when frying. I ate only a handful before composting the rest since I knew it was so unhealthy for me.

The base (just the potato mash) was actually really delicious, not gonna lie. So in the future, it would be fun to make some more interesting mashed potato blends without the whole croquette part. Maybe I’ll use less butter too. But I can conclude that green cabbage, green onion, leek, and potato do make a good combo.

That could be a very fun way to incorporate different starches/carbs into my diet. I am a lowkey carb hater and easily get tired of the same carb so fun mashed potato combos could get added into my carb rotation. I also do kinda like soft/soggy textures too.

I wouldn’t have gotten to this profoundly deep life realization without this potato journey, so thank you Emily.
